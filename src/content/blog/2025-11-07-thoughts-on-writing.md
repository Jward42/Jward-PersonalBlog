Writing is still the cleanest way I know to hear myself think.

When a note stays in my head, it remains vague, flattering, and unfinished. The moment it lands on a page, it becomes something I can actually look at, doubt, reshape, or keep. That friction is useful. It slows thought down just enough to make it honest.

I do not think of writing as publishing first. I think of it as arranging attention. Sometimes it becomes an essay. Sometimes it remains a margin note. Either way, the process leaves a small map behind, and I keep returning to those maps.
